# Field Notes Design System

## Visual Direction

The site should feel like a cross between a compliance dossier, a newsroom proof, and a systems notebook.

## Ingredients

- Warm paper backgrounds rather than pure white.
- Dense sans-serif headlines paired with a literary serif body face.
- Monospaced status chips, timestamps, and evidence labels.
- Red, amber, and cyan as signal colors rather than decorative accents.

## Layout Rules

- Let the home page feel editorial, not app-like.
- Preserve generous breathing room around article intros.
- Keep rails useful: references and a reader-facing "why it matters" brief.

## Image Language

- Prefer OpenAI GPT Image article plates when available, with the procedural renderer as the deterministic fallback.
- Treat `docs/image-style-guide.md` as the source of truth for article image prompts.
- Use abstract editorial plates, not cliche robots or glowing brains.
- Bridge two perceptual worlds: how an LLM might visualize its own existence as latent space, recursive attention, memory fragments, probability fields, and tool-call traces; and how humans imagine it from the outside as a governed black box, audit artifact, compliance object, or collaborator behind glass.
- Favor liminal interfaces: one side can feel interior, nonhuman, distributed, and self-modeling; the other side should feel human-facing, legible, paper-based, institutional, and accountable.
- Favor chart-room geometry, signal traces, layered paper blocks, forensic annotations, translucent model-space grids, and evidence surfaces.
- Every image should read as specific to the article's argument.
- Never personify the system as a robot, face, glowing brain, or mystical oracle.

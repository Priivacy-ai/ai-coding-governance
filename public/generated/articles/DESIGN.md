# Governance Image Design Brief

This directory contains the canonical reference images for the main governance-house visual lane.

These images define the site's recurring governance idiom: the **translation chamber**. They are the visual counterpart to the site's core argument that AI coding systems become valuable only when ambiguous model activity is translated into accountable, reviewable engineering evidence.

## Purpose

Use this lane for the primary governance articles about:

- auditability
- context engineering
- deployment risk
- compliance deadlines
- provenance
- failure containment
- control surfaces
- engineering accountability

This lane should feel more atmospheric, architectural, and story-driven than the flatter weekly-tools lane.

## Exact Idiom

- Warm ivory paper ground with subtle drafting texture
- Charcoal and graphite structural lines, circles, grids, and measurement marks
- Muted cyan or teal traces representing model-state, context flow, or tool-call paths
- Signal-red and amber gates, brackets, seals, rails, and interrupt marks representing review authority
- Layered paper dossiers, ledgers, tabs, and evidence stacks on the human-accountable side
- Transitional membranes, apertures, thresholds, cliffs, filters, seams, frames, and chambers between model-space and governance-space
- A stronger sense of architectural depth than the weekly-tools lane: translucent planes, stacked layers, portals, radial pressure, suspended sheets, and spatial staging
- A controlled feeling of uncertainty: traces should be partially resolved, filtered, compressed, or interrupted rather than becoming perfectly clean

## Subject Matter

The translation chamber is a visual grammar, not a fixed composition.

Acceptable article-specific forms include:

- radial pressure chambers
- layered vertical filters
- suspended evidence planes
- threshold gates
- cliff-edge crossings
- fractured provenance routes
- dossier assembly tables
- archival audit stacks

The governing idea is always the same:

1. ambiguous nonhuman activity begins in model-space
2. it crosses a boundary, gate, membrane, or inspection surface
3. it emerges as partial but accountable evidence for human review

## Avoid

- No robots, faces, humanoids, or mascots
- No neon sci-fi or cyberpunk treatment
- No glossy app mockups
- No flat logo collages
- No ordinary office scenes
- No readable interface copy unless a rare article specifically requires diagram text and it is approved deliberately
- No drift into the flatter weekly-tools poster idiom unless the article is intentionally about the tools lane

## Relationship To The Tools Lane

Do not confuse this with `/public/generated/tools/DESIGN.md`.

The weekly-tools lane is flatter, more poster-like, and about governed collaboration surfaces.

This governance-house lane is deeper, more liminal, and about translation from model interiority into accountable evidence.

## Agent Note

If you are generating a new governance article image pair:

1. Read `docs/image-style-guide.md`.
2. Read this file.
3. Select one seeded-random image from the canonical files in this directory as the style-continuity reference.
4. Use the guidance: `like this, but adjusted to the new article's narrative`.
5. Inspect the selected reference image and at least two other canonical files in this directory.
6. Keep the phrase `translation chamber` in the prompt.
7. Preserve the idiom, but vary the composition so the site does not become repetitive.
